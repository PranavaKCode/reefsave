Volunteer Training

Volunteers seeking to assist in field testing in August of 2026 are required to complete the course for the position they wish to perform. Volunteers who work the full week may be entitled to up to $800 reimbursement.

Underwater Robot Operator

The first lesson is to read and understand the operation and controls for the underwater robot. The robot has a claw, the operator must understand how the claw works, and must subsequently operate the robot at least 3 times to become a certified Underwater Robot Operator.

Requirements: In addition to the Lesson Fee, to complete the course you must have three In-Field Operation Lessons using the Underwater Robot, each costing $75 US Dollars.

Schedule Three Open Water Field Lessons in South Florida. The In-Field Training will require the scheduling of the boat that will go into the Intercostal and into the Atlantic Ocean.

For the FAD Aggregation tests in August of 2026, there can be a maximum of 2 Underwater Robot Operators. After you complete Lesson 1, please Contact Us.

Data Manager

The Data Manager is required to be aboard the boat used for testing in August 2026 for the entire week. Lesson 1 outlines the types of data to be collected and the manner in which each type of data is obtained.

Good Location Prediction is the Goal. Includes: Forms, Explanation of Field Names, and Procedures used for Data Collection Objectives and Back-up Data Storage.

Data Back-Up is Mission Critical. It is the Responsibility of the Data Manager to confirm that all the resources and equipment needed is available.

Multi-day Field Projects Require Clear Daily Logs. Capturing data in real time and recording impressions and notes may be forgotten when the data is reviewed weeks later.

Explain where you put the Data. Protection of the Data is critical to the step of Raising Capital to create a B-Corp Franchise System.
